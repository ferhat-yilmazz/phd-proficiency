## Objective

A comprehensive introduction to artificial neural networks and machine learning.
Alongside fundamentals — biological motivation, types of learning, perceptrons,
and backpropagation — it builds a foundation in advanced topics: Bayesian
classifiers, dimensionality reduction, support vector machines, convolutional
neural networks, reinforcement learning, and autoencoders.

## Content

Artificial neural network (ANN) representations and biological motivation;
knowledge representation and types of learning; perceptrons, multilayer
perceptrons, and the backpropagation algorithm; Bayesian decision theory and
Bayesian classifiers; the importance of good features — dimensionality reduction
and principal component analysis (PCA); linear discriminant functions and support
vector machines (SVM); convolutional neural networks (CNN); reinforcement
learning; autoencoders.

## Learning outcomes

1. Explain the relationship between biological nervous systems and artificial neural network models.
2. Describe the basic principles of ANNs and how they are inspired by the workings of real brains.
3. Identify and discuss the key factors affecting effective learning and generalization in neural-network systems.
4. Implement a variety of commonly used neural-network architectures.
5. Evaluate data feature-selection methods for classification tasks and apply statistical analysis techniques.
