## Objective

The course is built on relating the tools of linear algebra to state variables
and on formulating the mathematics used to solve specific systems. The study of
state variables and the characterization of systems matters both for researchers
doing theoretical control work and for practitioners who need to follow the
control literature.

## Content

Generating state-variable definitions; vector spaces; transformations; solutions
of linear systems; eigenvalues as a basis; rank and nullity of a matrix;
generalized eigenvectors; Jordan forms; Cayley–Hamilton techniques; direct
solutions for linear dynamics; discrete-time systems; controllability and
observability; controllability/observability tests for continuous-time systems;
canonical forms and stability.

## Learning outcomes

1. Identify, formulate, and solve linear control problems with analytical reasoning, and assimilate linear control systems.
2. Design and implement multivariable linear control systems.
3. Analyze the controllability and observability properties of systems.
