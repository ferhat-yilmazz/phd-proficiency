## Objective

Give students a working command of the mathematical modeling of physical
systems: deriving models, studying linear system dynamics, and analyzing
transient- and steady-state responses through the solution of differential
equations.

## Content

Introduction to mathematical modeling and system identification; mechanical
systems; heat and fluid-flow models; linearization and scaling; electrical
systems and system elements; mathematical models of electrical circuits;
electrical–mechanical analogies; analysis of mechanical, thermal, and hydraulic
systems via equivalent electrical circuits; linear nth-order differential-equation
models; first-order systems with their step, ramp, and frequency responses;
first-order electrical and hybrid systems; second-order systems (mechanical and
electrical).

## Learning outcomes

1. Demonstrate in-depth knowledge of mathematical modeling and build mathematical models of industrial systems.
2. Understand the concept of system response.
3. Interpret the behavior of higher-order systems.
