## Objective

Viewed broadly, Linear System Theory is for most students the second graduate
course on linear state-space equations; for some it is the first, and for others
it sits in between. The course builds, for students of widely differing
backgrounds, the foundation in linear systems they will meet in their research.
Its core material is the theory of linear **time-varying** continuous-time
systems; results for time-invariant linear systems are treated as special cases.

## Content

Mathematical notation and fundamentals (vectors, matrices, matrix algebra);
state-space representation and linearization; solutions of the state equations;
existence and uniqueness of solutions; properties of the state-transition matrix;
internal stability; Lyapunov stability; controllability and observability;
realizability and minimal realization; input–output stability; controller and
observer forms; linear (state) feedback; the disturbance-rejection problem.

## Learning outcomes

1. Interpret the concepts of internal stability, bounded-input bounded-output (BIBO) stability, and Lyapunov stability.
2. Understand the concepts of realizability and minimal realization.
3. Design a state observer and solve the disturbance-rejection problem.
